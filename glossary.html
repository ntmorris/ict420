Glossary

1 Sulla Consul of Rome as of the mentioned civil war
2 Marius Former Consul of Rome. Enacted the famous 'Marian Reforms'. Uncle of Caesar by marriage.
3 Aegean Sea The sea between modern Greece ("Achaea") and Turkey ("Asia")
4 Military Tribune Equivalent to a modern middle-ranking officer. Tribunes commanded portions of the army, but were subordinates of those appointed by the senate. This position was often held by young senatorial hopefuls.
5 Quaestor A supervisor of the Roman Republic's treasury and finances.
6 Curule Aedile Aediles maintained public holdings and regulated public festivals. Curule Aediles were one of two distinctions of Aedile, one open to both commoners and nobility.
7 Pontifex Maximus The Chief High Priest of Rome. The highest position of Roman religion.
8 Catilinarian Conspiracy The Second Catilinarian Conspiracy was a conspiracy by Catiline (Catilina) to overthrow the consulship of Cicero. Caesar was accused of being a member.
9 Crassus Crassus was an extremely wealthy Roman, who was part of the First Triumvirate. He supported Caesar politically and financially.
10 Pompey Pompey was a significant statesman. He was a member of the First Triumvirate and later mortal enemy of Caesar's. He was Caesar's opposition in Caesar's civil war.
11 Imperator This was a title a general's army could bestow upon him.
12 Triumph A celebration of a general's victory. This usually consisted of an elaborate march of the army, centering on the general himself.
13 First Triumvirate An informal alliance of the three most powerful Romans at the time: Caesar, Crassus, and Pompey. They used this alliance to carry each other's political will. This alliance broke down, resulting in Caesar's civil war.
14 Gaul Gaul was what is now modern France. At the start of Caesar's governorship, Gaul was small. Caesar then conquered all of what is now France.
15 Mark Antony He distringuished himself at the Battle of Alesia. Noticing this, Caesar propelled Antony's career. Antony maintained a position at Caesar's right hand for the remainder of Caesar's life.
16 Rubicon River A river in Northern Italy. It was used as a line to demarcate how far armies had to remain from the Roman capital.
17 Dictator An official position which was given to someone, usually an incumbent Consul, by the Senate in a time of great trouble to Rome.
18 Octavian Caesar's grand-nephew and adoptive son. Caesar declared him his heir. Octavian went on to be a member of the Second Triumvirate and eventual first emperor of Rome, known as Augustus Caesar.
